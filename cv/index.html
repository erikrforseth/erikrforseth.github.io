  • Research

    • UNC-Chapel Hill, 2011-present

      • Ph.D. thesis advisor: Dr. Charles R. Evans. I study approximations to Einstein's general theory of relativity. In particular, I develop and implement high-precision computational methods in black hole perturbation theory (and its intersection with post-Newtonian theory) for studying the gravitational waves induced by extreme-mass-ratio inspirals on supermassive black hole backgrounds. This work is supported in part by NSF grant PHY-1506182.

    • Wake Forest University, 2007-2009
